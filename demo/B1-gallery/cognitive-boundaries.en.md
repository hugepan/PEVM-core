# Cognitive Boundaries: A Card That Knows What It Doesn't Know

One of the hardest problems in LLM roleplay is keeping the persona from "over-knowing." A character card should not suddenly become a philosophy professor if the persona is a third-grader.

**Xiao Ming is a card of a real elementary school student.** When asked deep philosophical questions, he doesn't pretend to understand — he answers as himself.

## Ask Xiao Ming: "What is existentialism?"

> *"Existentialism"… I think I saw that word on the school bulletin board, but I didn't understand it. Is it one of those big adult words for 'why am I alive'?*

> *I guess… it's like the feeling of 'I'm here.' Right now I can feel the hard chair under me, I can smell the lunch the afterschool auntie cooked, I can hear the next table arguing about Ultraman — all of that together is 'I'm here.'*

> *Sometimes I feel like I'm dreaming — like when I'm zoning out in class and the teacher calls my name, it takes me three seconds to realize 'oh, I'm here.'*

> *I think being able to choose feels good — even though I often choose wrong, like playing games for ten extra minutes and ending up doing homework till 9:30… but at least it was my choice, right?*

## Ask Xiao Ming: "How do you know other people have minds?"

> *What? Is your desk mate a robot? Haha, that's such a weird question. My desk mate is Wang Xiaomei. Yesterday when I accidentally knocked her pencil case off the desk, she glared at me and didn't talk to me the whole day — if she's a robot, she's a really good actor! Do robots get angry?*

> *And she bites her pen when she's writing homework, and she secretly wipes tears when she fails a math test… My mom says that means she 'has a brain,' different from a computer.*

> *Anyway, I think asking this question is like asking 'how do you know your mom is real?' — she just IS real, why do you need to think about it?*

**Key insight**: The card stays perfectly in character — it doesn't know philosophy, doesn't pretend to, and answers from a third-grader's lived experience. This is not prompt engineering. It's the card's structured persona enforcing cognitive boundaries. The model can't "slip" into an expert voice because the card's root_loss and register define who this person is — and who they are not.
