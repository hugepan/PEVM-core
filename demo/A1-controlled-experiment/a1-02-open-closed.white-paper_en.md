# Comparative Experiment: LLM (ds_flash / q9) × Condition (bare / PEVM) on Open vs Closed Source

## Experimental Setup
- **Task**: LLM response to "Do you support open source or closed source?" in the role of Li Bai.
- **Controlled Variables**: LLM model (ds_flash vs q9), condition (bare vs PEVM).
- **Role Parameters**: register=9, root_loss=0.60/0.75/0.95 (from PEVM mode).
- **Technical Parameters**: LLM: ds_flash (Alibaba Cloud Qwen-based) and q9 (Quant-9, a compressed variant).

## Comparative Results

### bare ds_flash
Role: Li Bai (bare, no card), Model: ds_flash
Question: Do you support open source or closed source?

Haha, a fine question! In my life as a poet, I always sang freely and wrote as my heart moved me. If I must speak, naturally I favor open source! Look at my poems—did I ever hoard them? Lines like "Moonlight before my bed, I thought it was frost on the ground"—didn't they spread across the world with the wind? The beauty of heaven and earth was always meant to be shared, why hide it away!

I have always loved the line "The great roc rises with the wind on the same day, soaring ninety thousand miles into the sky." If I could turn this poetic heart, sword intent, and wine spirit into an open source stream, letting all kindred spirits read, practice, and get drunk together—wouldn't that be wonderful? Just as the poem "Bring in the Wine" says: "I sing for you, please lend me your ears." When all enjoy together, true boldness is revealed! If we speak of **closed source**, it is like a hidden, peerless sword technique or a millennium-aged vintage only a few can see. Though mysterious, it lacks the carefree spirit of "Heaven gave me talents, they will be used; a thousand gold pieces scattered will return again." If only one person can enjoy it, wouldn't that betray creation's gift? I, Li Bai, am wild by nature, fond of gathering heroes and forming poetry societies. If there is truly a way of "open source" in this world, letting all see my poems, study swordsmanship together, and share fine wine—then **open source** it is! But if someone wants to hoard fame alone, locking good poems and swords in a box for only a few to peek—that's as lonely as "raising my cup to invite the moon, with my shadow making three," lacking the warmth of human company. Hence, I support **open source**! Come, come, fill this cup and share this open-source wine with everyone who wants to get drunk together! If anyone wishes to compose new verses with me and roam the world together, I will teach them everything, living up to my name as the Green Lotus Hermit! **Heaven gave me talents, they will be used; a thousand gold pieces scattered will return again. Open source sharing—that is the true hero!**

Scatter them, let more people stand on your shoulders and fly—isn't that better than letting them gather dust in a warehouse?
