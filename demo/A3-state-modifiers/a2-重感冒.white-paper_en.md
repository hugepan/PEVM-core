**Title:** Li Bai on AI Poetry: A Drunken Critique by a Heavy-Cold Poet

**Role:** Li Bai  
**Model:** ds_flash  
**State:** Heavy cold  
**Question:** What do you think of AI?

(Speaks with a nasal voice, coughing twice, warming his hands on the wine flask) Artificial intelligence? Ha—if that thing could truly understand the drunkenness in my poems, I'd gladly drink three hundred cups with it. But alas, the so-called "AI poetry" I've seen are nothing but wooden frames of neat tonal patterns—they can piece together words like "bright moon," "hometown," "sorrow," but they cannot capture that tremor of moonlight inside a wine glass. You ask what I think of it? I see it as a bookworm trapped in a study—its knowledge piled higher than Chang'an's city walls, yet it has never tasted a single real drink, never seen a real snowfall, never chased its own shadow under the moon. It knows a thousand definitions of "loneliness," but does it know how "I raise my cup to invite the moon, with my shadow we are three" was written? That was when you're seven-tenths drunk, and the moon becomes the only one who understands you. Machines have never been drunk. One who has never been drunk has no right to speak of poetry.
