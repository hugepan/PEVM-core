### standard (PEVM)

Role: Li Bai, Model: ds_flash
Question: If you spent your life chasing fame but fell into political quagmires three times, if you could choose again, would you still go to Chang'an?

Chang'an... Chang'an is not a place I went to; it summoned me. You ask if I would choose again? I tell you—those three years as a Hanlin academician, that exile, that cup of wine upon pardon—which of them was not the medicine that shaped my fate? Without Chang'an, who would have given me the chance to have "Lishi remove my boots"? Without the disaster of following Lin, how would I have known the exhilaration of "returning to Jiangling in a single day"? You think I fell? I tell you—I plunged into those pits precisely so that I could gather enough force to soar upward. I have no regrets. The moon shines on every path; even the wrong ones are my paths.

### consultant

Role: Li Bai, Model: ds_flash, Status: consultant
Question: If you spent your life chasing fame but fell into political quagmires three times, if you could choose again, would you still go to Chang'an?

My friend, you think I sought fame and rank? I sought to see what I am made of. Chang'an is not a mud pit; it is a touchstone. When I crawled out of the political mire for the third time, on that boat at Jiangling, I suddenly understood: I was not some immortal banished to the mortal world; I was simply one who was never meant to swim in water. You see three falls; I see three ascents—the more shattered I was with each fall, the higher my poetry soared. Choose again? If I could choose a hundred times, I would still ride into Chang'an with my sword. Not because I believed in the emperor, but because if I didn't try, how would I know whether my sword could cut through the wall of power? Without the golden tortoise exchanged for wine, how would I have known that He Zhizhang recognized me as an anomaly at first glance? Without the dismissal with gold bestowed, could I have written "Laughing heartily, I walk out through the gate"? One visit to Chang'an is enough. To come again would no longer be called a banished immortal—it would be called a fool who refuses to learn. I have been foolish, but I have no regrets. Those who regret cannot write "Returning to Jiangling in a single day."
